Hedtek was a software house in the period July 2007 to Jul 2016.

Besides straightforward commercial work for clients building, extending and running their systems, Hedtek was awarded more than Â£1M to perform applied research for various UK government-funded organisations.

Hedtek developed Gradient; an electronic assessment system for universities and high schools. Gradient primarily focuses on ease-of-use for students and assessors. Hedtek retains its interest in further exploitation of Gradient's patent-protected technology.

Hedtek also provided technical and strategic consultancy services.

Manchester based, Hedtek was closed as a software house in mid 2016 to enable a move from rain-drenched Manchester to sunnier climes in London.

From that time, the company has served as a vehicle for contracting and consultancy activities, together with some open source development work.

In a twist in geographic location, Hedtek's principal is now the CTO of a Manchester-based startup, Prevayl, albeit working in the new normal -- fully remotely.

Hedtek remains a vehicle for various activities; currently CNC machine development for the commercial market.
